# Goal: Lose Weight / Get Lean

## GIP3 (Retatrutide) — customers call it "Reda" or "Reta"
The strongest weight-loss compound ever made. It hits three different hunger and metabolism switches at the same time (GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon). In a real human study, people lost about **24% of their body weight in 11 months**. That beats Ozempic. That beats Mounjaro.

**Who buys it:** People who want to lose 30+ pounds. People who plateaued on Ozempic or Mounjaro. People tired of side effects from those drugs.

**Sales note:** This is our flagship. Best margins, biggest demand, repeat orders. Most people start with the 10mg vial ($100) and trade up to 30mg ($220) once they trust it works. Push the upgrade — per-mg gets cheaper as size goes up.

## Cagrilintide
Cuts hunger without the gut side effects of the GLPs. Smoother appetite control — less nausea, less "food noise" in your head. People often stack it with GIP3 for stronger results, or use it alone if they can't handle Ozempic-class drugs.

**Who buys:** Anyone who couldn't tolerate Ozempic. Stack-builders pairing it with GIP3.

## AOD-9604
Targets fat loss specifically — doesn't mess with blood sugar or muscle. Milder than retatrutide. Works on stubborn body fat (belly, love handles).

**Who buys:** People who don't want to commit to a GLP. Lower entry price.

## MOTS-c
Fat loss plus an energy boost. Comes from mitochondrial DNA — works at the cellular level, not the gut. People feel more energy on it, not less.

**Who buys:** Athletes, biohackers, people who hate appetite-suppressant drugs.

## 5-Amino-1MQ
Blocks an enzyme that tells the body to store fat. People often pair it with NAD+ for energy.

**Who buys:** People already pretty lean trying to push further. Body recomp crowd.

# Goal: Heal an Injury / Recover Faster

## BPC-157 — customers call it "BPC"
The healing peptide. Speeds up healing of tendons, ligaments, joints, gut lining, just about any soft tissue. Made famous in CrossFit, powerlifting, and biohacker circles.

**Who buys:** Sports injuries. Tendonitis. Post-surgery. Leaky gut. IBS. Chronic joint pain. People who hurt themselves doing something stupid and don't want to wait three months to be normal again.

**Important:** We sell injectable AND oral tablets (tablets are upsell-only). The tablets target the gut specifically — BPC was originally found in stomach acid, so the gut is its native home.

## Pentadecarginine *(upsell-only / hidden)*
A more stable version of BPC-157 — same effects, lasts longer in the body. Fewer injections to get the same outcome.

**Who buys:** Existing BPC users who hate frequent injecting.

## TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4 fragment)
Faster muscle and tendon healing. Works through different mechanisms than BPC, which is why people stack them together.

**Who buys:** Athletes with rotator cuff issues, knee problems, anyone serious about injury rehab.

## BPC-157 + TB-500 Blend
The "Wolverine stack." Combines both for major injury work. Most popular blend on the site after the GH stacks.

**Who buys:** Torn ligaments. Post-surgery patients. People with nagging chronic injuries that physical therapy isn't fixing.

## Recovery Tri-Blend (BPC + TB-500 + GHK-Cu) *(upsell-only / hidden)*
Same as the Wolverine stack but adds GHK-Cu for skin and scar healing on top.

## ARA-290
Nerve pain and chronic pain that doesn't respond to anything else. Comes from EPO (the cycling-doping hormone) research — they pulled out the part that helps nerves without the part that thickens blood.

**Who buys:** Diabetic neuropathy. Chronic pain patients. Post-injury nerve damage. Niche but loyal.

## KPV
Stops mast cells from going crazy. Calms gut inflammation. People with weird food sensitivities, allergies, or MCAS use it.

**Who buys:** People who react to half the foods they eat. MCAS patients. Crohn's/colitis-style symptoms.

## Larazotide *(upsell-only / hidden)*
Tightens the gut lining — the actual mechanism behind "leaky gut." People with autoimmune issues use it.

**Who buys:** Functional medicine patients, autoimmune crowd.

# Goal: Build Muscle / Boost Growth Hormone

## CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin Blend — "the GH stack"
The classic growth hormone stack. CJC tells the brain to **make** growth hormone. Ipa tells the brain to **release** it. People feel: better sleep, better recovery from workouts, less body fat, more muscle, better skin. Way cheaper and safer than buying actual HGH on the black market.

**Who buys:** Guys 35+. Lifters. Biohackers. Anyone who heard their friend say "I started feeling like I was 25 again." Probably the second-most-shipped blend after GIP3.

## Tesamorelin — customers call it "Tessa"
Stronger growth hormone releaser than CJC. Specifically targets visceral belly fat — the deep belly fat that won't go away with diet. FDA-approved for HIV-related belly fat, so there's serious clinical data behind it.

**Who buys:** Older men with belly fat that won't budge. Premium customers who outgrew the CJC/Ipa stack.

## Tesamorelin + Ipamorelin Blend
Same idea as CJC/Ipa but with the more powerful Tesa. Premium tier of the GH stack lineup.

**Who buys:** Experienced GH-stack users upgrading their protocol.

## Ipamorelin (alone)
The "clean" GH releaser. Doesn't bump cortisol or prolactin like other secretagogues. Used in custom stacks.

**Who buys:** Sophisticated stackers who want to dose CJC and Ipa separately, or people sensitive to other peptides.

## Sermorelin
The mildest, most beginner-friendly GH releaser. The introduction-level option.

**Who buys:** First-time peptide users. Anti-aging clinic patients.

## CJC-1295 (no DAC) (alone)
Sold separately for people who want to dose CJC and Ipa on their own custom schedule.

# Goal: Look Younger / Better Skin / Hair Growth

## GHK-Cu
The copper peptide. Builds collagen. Heals skin. Reverses sun damage. Regrows hair. Used both injected (whole-body effect) and topical (face, scalp). The 50mg vial is for face/scar use; 100mg for serious anti-aging protocols.

**Who buys:** Women into anti-aging. Men with hair loss. People with surgery scars. Dermatology patients trying to avoid prescription routes.

## PTD-DBM *(upsell-only / hidden)*
Hair regrowth — works through a different pathway (Wnt) than minoxidil and finasteride. So people add it on top of those. Newer compound, fewer studies, but enthusiasts swear by it.

**Who buys:** People who tried Rogaine and Propecia and want another lever. Usually applied topically.

## Epithalon
Tells cells to keep their telomeres long. Telomeres are the protective caps on the ends of your DNA — they shorten as you age, and short telomeres are basically the "aging clock." Russian researchers have decades of animal data showing it extends life.

**Who buys:** Hard-core anti-aging crowd. Longevity enthusiasts. Not for newbies — buyers usually know exactly what they want.

## Glow Blend (BPC + TB-500 + GHK-Cu)
All-in-one skin, recovery, and healing combo in a single vial. $200 — premium price point.

**Who buys:** High-end customer who wants the whole package without managing three vials.

## FOXO4-DRI *(upsell-only / hidden)*
Senolytic. Kills "zombie cells" — old cells that should die but won't, and that drive aging-related disease. Real research backing it from a Dutch lab.

**Who buys:** Serious longevity nerds. Niche.

# How to handle the "does it work?" question

Two truthful answers, depending on the compound:

**Real clinical trials** (high confidence): GIP3 (retatrutide), Tesamorelin, Thymosin Alpha-1, SS-31, NAD+, PT-141, Cagrilintide.

**Strong anecdotal + animal data + smaller human trials** (good confidence): BPC-157, TB-500, GHK-Cu, CJC/Ipa, Epithalon, MOTS-c, Selank, Semax, DSIP, Kisspeptin-10, KPV, ARA-290, VIP.

**Mostly anecdotal + early research** (be honest): PTD-DBM, FOXO4-DRI, P21, Adamax, Larazotide.

When you don't know — say "the research is still developing" and point them at the product page, the blog, or their own provider. Don't make stuff up.

# What NOT to write down

In email, chat, SMS — never write:

- Specific human dosing protocols ("take 250mcg twice a week")
- Disease cure claims ("this cures Crohn's")
- Comparisons to prescription drugs phrased as medical claims ("works like Ozempic without the side effects")
- Reconstitution instructions framed for human use
- Stacking advice for personal use

Verbal conversations with educated buyers have more latitude. Written records get screenshot and become FDA evidence. Keep written replies focused on: product specs, shipping, payment, account issues, links to product pages.
